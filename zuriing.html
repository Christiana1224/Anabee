Nelson Mandela

A South Africa anti-apartheid activist who served as the first president of South Africa from 1994-1999. He was the country's first black Head of State and the first elected in a fully representative democractic election. He won the Noble Peace Prize in 1993.

Timeline of Nelson Mandela

  1. July 18, 1918, Nelson Mandela was born in Mvezo, Eastern Cape province, South Africa. His birth name is Rolihlahla.
  2. In 1939, he enrolled at the University College of Fort Hare in Alice, Eastrn Cape, for a bachelor of arts degree.
  3. In 1940, he was expelled from university for joining in a protest boycott.
  4. In 1941, he escaped an arranged marriage, becomes a mine secutiry officer; starts articles at the law firm Witkin, Sidelsky and Eidelman.
  5. Mandela marries Evelyn Ntoko Mase, his first wife, in October 5, 1944.
  6. He was awarded the Noble Peace Prize in December 10, 1993.
  7. In 2003, Mandela lends his name to the 46664 Aids awareness and fundraising campaign, named for his former prison number.
  8. July 2001, Mandela was diagnosed and successfully treated for prostrate cancer.
  9. In June 27, 2011, His book Nelson Mandela By Himself: The Authorized Book of Quotations was launched.
  10. In December 5, 2013, Mandela passed away at his home in Houghton at the age of 95. An official 10-day period of mourning was declared in South Africa.
  11. During his lifetime, Mandela received more than 695 awards.

Here is a little biography of Nelson Mandela:

Rolihlahla Mandela was born into the Madiba clan in the village of Mvezo, in the Eastern Cape, on 18 July 1918. His mother was Nonqaphi Nosekeni and his father was Nkosi Mphakanyiswa Gadla Mandela, principal counsellor to the Acting King of the Thembu people, Jongintaba Dalindyebo. In 1930, when he was 12 years old, his father died and the young Rolihlahla became a ward of Jongintaba at the Great Place in Mqhekezweni1.
